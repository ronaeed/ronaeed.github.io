Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Percentage?

A percentage is a unit used to represent a ratio or a part of a whole, and it is defined as one out of a hundred.

The use of percentages allows for a convenient comparison between different groups regardless of their size.

How to Calculate a Percentage?

The formula is simple: divide the part by the whole and then multiply by 100.

For example, if 20 out of 50 apples are red, the percentage of red apples can be calculated as follows:

\( \left( \frac{20}{50} \right) \times 100 = 40\% \)

So, 40% of the apples are red.

How to Calculate Percentage Increase?

To calculate percentage increase, first determine the difference between the two numbers you are comparing. Divide this difference by the original number and then multiply by 100.

For example, if a product's price increases from $50 to $60, the percentage increase is calculated as

\( \left( \frac{\$60 - \$50}{\$50} \right) \times 100 = 20\% \)
